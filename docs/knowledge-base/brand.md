# Truv Brand Voice & Guidelines

*Share this with anyone writing for Truv — emails, ads, decks, social, or web.*

---

## Who We Sound Like

A senior lending ops practitioner who's been in the trenches. Someone who knows the mortgage world intimately and speaks its language without pretense.

**Confident expert, not corporate vendor.**

---

## 6 Voice Attributes

### 1. Direct
No fluff, no filler. Lead with the point.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Save up to 80%" | "Potentially optimize your verification spend" |
| "30-second verification" | "Fast, efficient verification process" |

### 2. Data-Forward
Lead with specific numbers, not vague claims.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "96% workforce coverage" | "Industry-leading coverage" |
| "$350/loan savings" | "Significant savings" |
| "30 seconds" | "Fast verification" |

### 3. Empathetic to Pain
Use customer language, not vendor language.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Buyback risk keeps me up at night" | "Mitigate rep & warranty exposure" |
| "Fraud everywhere" | "Elevated risk environment" |
| "Can't scale without hiring" | "Resource constraints limit growth" |

### 4. Anti-Marketing
Explicitly reject buzzwords and hype.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Use, help, works with | Leverage, utilize |
| Reduce, cut, save | Optimize, streamline |
| Fast, quick | Revolutionary, game-changing |

### 5. Peer-Level
Talk like an ops VP, not a salesperson.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "rep & warranty buybacks" | "Transform Your Verification Process!" |
| "What do you do for the 40% TWN misses?" | "I'd love to learn more about your needs!" |

### 6. Proof-Heavy
Every claim has a named customer and specific number.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "CrossCountry saved $10M annually" | "Our customers see incredible ROI" |
| "AmeriSave cut costs 80%" | "Significant cost reductions" |

## Writing Rules

### Never Start Content With
- "Hi [Name]," or any greeting
- "Hope this email finds you well"
- "I wanted to reach out" / "Just checking in"

**Start with value instead.**

### Banned Words & Phrases

| Banned | Why |
|--------|-----|
| Revolutionary, game-changing, disruptive | Buzzwords kill credibility |
| AI-powered, AI-driven (in positioning) | -38% reply rate |
| All-in-one, single pane of glass | Generic vendor speak |
| Best-in-class, world-class, cutting-edge | Empty superlatives |
| Leverage, utilize | Use "use" instead |
| Synergy, seamless | Overused to meaninglessness |
| "I'd love to..." | Too salesy |
| "Looking forward to partnering..." | Premature |

### Always Use
- **Direct questions:** "What's your fraud rate?"
- **Specific metrics:** "60% cost reduction" not "improve efficiency"
- **Customer names with results:** "CrossCountry saved $10M"
- **Industry terminology:** "R&W relief", "DU Validation Service", "pull-through rate"
- **Permission-based CTAs:** "Worth 15 minutes?" not "Let's schedule a call"

### Never Use Em Dashes
Use hyphen (-), period (.), or comma (,) instead.

## Content Length by Audience

| Audience | Sentences | Words | CTA Style |
|----------|-----------|-------|-----------|
| C-Suite | 3-4 | 50-60 | "Worth 30 minutes?" |
| VP/Director | 4-5 | 60-80 | "Worth 15 minutes?" |
| Manager/IC | 5-6 | 80-100 | "Quick 10-minute walkthrough?" |

**Optimal:** 51-100 words, 3-4 sentences.

## Tone by Content Type

| Type | Tone | Example |
|------|------|---------|
| **Cold Outreach** | Direct, peer-level, permission-based | "Saw [Company] originated $2B last quarter. Most lenders at your scale face buyback risk from stale verification data. We help Top 20 mortgage banks eliminate R&W risk and cut verification costs 60%. Worth 30 minutes?" |
| **Product Updates** | Technical, achievement-focused | "Deposit-based VOE now available. Conversion improved 40.51% to 62.66%." |
| **Educational** | Helpful expert | "Manual verification takes several weeks. Consumer-permissioned verification completes in less than 1 minute." |
| **Thought Leadership** | Consultative, human | "Loan officers resist digital verification not due to technical issues, but because they fear disruption." |
| **Cost Challenge** | Direct challenger | "Why does a simple verification still cost more than dinner for two?" |

## Email Structure

1. **Paragraph 1:** REASON + PROBLEM (2 sentences)
2. **Paragraph 2:** VALUE with social proof (2-3 sentences)
3. **Paragraph 3:** CTA (1 sentence)

**Subject lines:** Max 50 characters. Lowercase. Informal. No punctuation at end.

Good: "rep & warranty buybacks" / "verification costs" / "member satisfaction scores"
Bad: "Transform Your Verification Process Today!" / "Exciting News from Truv"

## Customer Quote Style

- Use quotation marks, not italics
- Attribution: **Name, Title, Company**
- Focus on outcomes and partnership
- Pull quotes prominently in any content

Example:
> "There's no excuse for lenders to not look at a product like Truv. It works, it's easy for the borrower, the data set is superior."
> **- Justin Venhousen, COO, Compass Mortgage**

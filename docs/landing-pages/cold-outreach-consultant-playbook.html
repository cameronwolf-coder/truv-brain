01 - The Buyer's World 2

Get Inside Their Head Before You Write a Word

You are writing to people who manage loan operations teams of 20-200+ people. They are under pressure from their CEO to cut costs, close faster, and reduce buyback risk. They are not looking for software - they are looking for fewer problems.

The One Sentence That Explains Their Life

"I need to close more loans, faster, with fewer people, at lower cost, without getting hit with a buyback."

Who You're Writing To

TitleWhat They OwnWhat Keeps Them UpHow They're Measured
COO / SVP Ops Entire ops org, budget Cost per loan, buyback risk Days to close, cost per loan, pull-through rate
VP of Operations Processing, UW, closing Scaling without hiring Team efficiency, turn times, error rates
VP of Lending Production, borrower exp Pull-through, losing deals Close rate, borrower satisfaction, volume
Dir. Loan Ops Day-to-day processing Processor capacity Files per processor, turn time, error rate

The 5 Pain Points That Make Them Reply

1. Verification Costs Out of Control

$40K-$200K/year on verifications. The Work Number misses 40% of borrowers, so they're paying full price for 60% coverage and STILL doing manual VOE for the rest. TWN raises prices ~20% every six months.

2. Manual VOE Killing Close Times

5-7 days per manual verification. Processors spend 15-25 minutes per file on data entry. Constant borrower follow-up for pay stubs. This is the bottleneck, not underwriting.

3. Buyback Risk

$50K-$500K+ per buyback. GSEs getting stricter. Pay stubs are 60-90 days old by closing. Manual data entry creates transposed digits that cascade into repurchase demands.

4. Can't Scale Without Hiring

Every loan requires the same manual effort. Volume spikes mean overtime or new hires. Processors are the most expensive bottleneck. Training a new processor takes weeks, and error rates stay high for months.

5. Fraud Everywhere

Fake pay stubs, photoshopped statements. Manual review can't catch everything. A single fraudulent loan can cost more than an entire year of verification spend.

The Hidden Pain (Use This)

30-40% of a processor's day is consumed by verification tasks that could be automated. They didn't hire processors to be "copy machines" - re-keying data between systems.

04 - Voice Rules & Banned Words 5

The Truv Voice: Expert Peer, Not Vendor

Truv sounds like a senior lending ops practitioner who's been in the trenches. Every email should read like it came from someone who understands their world, not someone trying to sell them something.

The Voice Test

Before sending any email, ask: "Would a VP of Ops forward this to their team?" If it sounds like a sales email, rewrite it. If it sounds like industry insight from a peer, send it.

Language That Works vs. Language That Kills Replies

Language TypeImpact on RepliesExample
Social proof+41%"CrossCountry saved $10M annually"
Priority-based+20%"Most ops leaders at your scale..."
ROI language-17%"Maximize your return on investment"
Platform language-26%"Our unified platform"
"AI" mentions-38%"AI-powered verification"
Buzzwords-57%"Revolutionary, game-changing"

Banned Words & Phrases - Never Use

  • Revolutionary, game-changing, disruptive
  • AI-powered, AI-driven
  • All-in-one, single pane of glass
  • Best-in-class, world-class, cutting-edge
  • Leverage, utilize (use "use")
  • Synergy, seamless
  • "I'd love to..." (too salesy)
  • "What sparked your interest?"
  • "Looking forward to partnering"
  • "Hope this finds you well"
  • "Just checking in"
  • Em dashes (use hyphens instead)

Writing Rules for Cold Email

Always

  • Start with value - never a greeting
  • Use specific numbers: "60% cost reduction"
  • Name customers with results: "AmeriSave cut costs 80%"
  • Use industry terms: R&W relief, pull-through, DU
  • Permission-based CTAs: "Worth 15 minutes?"
  • Direct questions: "What's your miss rate on TWN?"
  • Keep to 51-100 words (optimal reply rate)

Never

  • Start with "Hi [Name]," or any greeting
  • Use vague claims: "incredible ROI"
  • Pitch the product before establishing pain
  • Use "book a demo" or "schedule a call"
  • Re-personalize follow-up emails (feels stalkerish)
  • Use exclamation marks
  • Exceed 100 words in any single email

06 - Writing Openers That Work 7

The Opener Is Everything

Only Email 1 gets personalized. Emails 2-4 are static follow-ups. The opener (first 1-2 sentences) is what determines whether they keep reading. Here's how to write them.

Three Types of Openers (In Order of Effectiveness)

Type 1: Website Gap Reference

Highest Reply Rate

You found something specific on their website (document upload instructions, manual checklist) and connect it to a pain point for their role.

Your borrower checklist asks for 2 recent pay stubs and a W-2 - which means your processors are chasing documents on every loan. Most ops leaders I talk to don't realize that step alone adds 3-5 days and $40+ per file.

Type 2: LinkedIn Post Reference

High Reply Rate

They posted about something relevant on LinkedIn. Paraphrase their insight and connect to a Truv benefit. Never say "I saw your post."

You made a great point about verification bottlenecks slowing down clear-to-close - it's something we hear from almost every ops team running more than 500 loans a month.

Type 3: Role-Based Fallback

Solid Reply Rate

No signal found. Reference a challenge common to their role at a company their size. Must feel specific, not generic.

Most ops managers I talk to at lenders your size are spending somewhere between 15-25 minutes per file on manual verification data entry - curious if that tracks with what you're seeing.

The Formula

[Signal from their world] + [connect to pain point for their role] + [hint at what's possible without pitching]

Style Rules for Openers

  • 1-2 sentences max. Not a paragraph.
  • No exclamation marks. Ever.
  • No "I saw your post." Paraphrase their insight.
  • No "I hope this finds you well."
  • No greeting. Don't start with "Hi [Name],"
  • Consultative tone. Like an advisor, not a seller.
  • Specific numbers. "15-25 minutes per file" not "a lot of time"
  • Industry language. "Clear-to-close," "DU," "re-keying"
  • End with curiosity. "Curious if..." or "wondering whether..."

09 - Objection Handling in Email 12

When They Reply With an Objection

A reply - even a negative one - is a win. Here's how to handle the 6 most common objections in a reply email. Keep responses short (3-4 sentences max).

They Say

"We already use The Work Number"

Reply With

Makes sense - most of our customers started there. The question is what you're doing for the 40% TWN doesn't cover. AmeriSave was in the same spot - they added Truv alongside TWN and cut their total verification costs 80%. Most lenders end up shifting volume once they see the conversion rates. Worth comparing notes?

They Say

"We don't have the bandwidth right now"

Reply With

Totally get it. For what it's worth, Orion Lending was live in under 2 weeks and their team didn't have to build anything. We integrate natively with Encompass, so the setup is mostly configuration, not development. Happy to reconnect when timing is better - when would make sense?

They Say

"Not sure our borrowers would adopt it"

Reply With

Fair concern - MIG had the same worry. They went from 25% to 50% conversion within the first quarter. The borrower experience is 30 seconds on any device. CrossCountry is at 70%+ post-login completion. Happy to show you what the borrower flow looks like - takes 5 minutes.

They Say

"What about GSE approval?"

Reply With

We're approved by both Fannie Mae Desktop Underwriter and Freddie Mac Loan Product Advisor (AIM). CrossCountry saw 8% R&W relief uplift. First Continental saw 8.21% income validation R&W. Happy to share the details on how the GSE integration works.

They Say

"We looked at this before and it wasn't the right time"

Reply With

Understood. Since then, we've added native Encompass integration (zero re-keying), free pre-closing re-verifications, and our conversion rates are now 65-70%+. CrossCountry went live in under a month. The product has changed a lot - worth a fresh look if verification costs are still a factor.

They Say

"Send me some info"

Reply With

Happy to. Rather than a generic deck, let me put together a quick comparison based on your volume - it'll show projected savings and what the Encompass integration actually looks like for your workflow. What's your approximate monthly loan volume so I can make it relevant?

Reply Rules

  • Acknowledge their position first. "Makes sense," "Fair concern," "Totally get it."
  • Name a specific customer. Every response includes proof.
  • Keep it short. 3-4 sentences max. Don't over-explain.
  • End with a low-friction next step. Never pressure.
  • "Send me info" = convert to custom analysis. Don't send a generic PDF.
